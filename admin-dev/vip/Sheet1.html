5.1 99.8% Uptime Guarantee.
In the event of Downtime as defined in 5.2, BC will at the Customer’s request, credit the Customer’s account for the failed service as described below.
If the total downtime in the calendar month is:
1. Greater than 0.7 hours but not exceeding 7.2 hours 2% of the Hourly recurring fee.
2. Greater than 7.2 hours but not exceeding 14.4 hours 4% of the Hourly recurring fee.
3. Greater than 14.4 hours but not exceeding 21.6 hours 8% of the Hourly recurring fee
4. Greater than 21.6 hours but not exceeding 28.8 hours 12% of the Hourly recurring fee.
5. Greater than 28.8 hours 50% of the Hourly recurring fee.
5.2 Downtime.
For the purposes of this Section, Downtime shall mean any interruption of ninety (90) seconds or more in the availability to users of the Service, but only if such interruption is due to either:
(a) Failure by BC to manage a situation so as to cause interruption in Network availability;
(b) A disruption in the connection between any such server and the Internet. For purposes of this Section, the Internet is deemed to consist of services that commence where BC transmits a Customer's content to BC carrier(s) at the BC border router port(s). Such carriers provide BC with private and dedicated bandwidth. BC undertakes no obligation for the circuit or link between BC facilities and such carrier’s services. If router packet loss is in excess of fifty percent (50%) and is sustained for one hundred and twenty (120) seconds or more, BC will classify this as an “outage.” If an “outage” continues for a time period of more than four (4) minutes, then such outage will be deemed Downtime;
(c) A fault in server hardware by which the Services are provided. If time between Customer's formal indication of service fault and BC's acknowledgement and response to begin resolution of the hardware fault exceeds four (4) hours, BC will classify this as Downtime.
(d) For purposes of this section, Resolution is either the replacement of faulty parts, or the provision of new system, or the escalation to professional services. In all cases BC will deem whether a service fault is due to hardware.
(e) For purposes of this section, Downtime as defined in section 5.2 begins at time the Customer reports service failure to BC. BlueCloud Support Ticketing System will manage the process.
5.3 Maintenance Windows.
In order to maintain and upgrade the The Service, BC will at times perform scheduled maintenance on its equipment. For avoidance of doubt, outages or performance degradation during scheduled maintenance windows as a result of router, switch or server maintenance, are not considered Downtime for purposes of this section. BC shall make all commercially reasonable efforts to provide the Customer with reasonable prior notification of all scheduled and emergency maintenance procedures.
